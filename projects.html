Projects

These are the main project areas from my AI in Construction and Facilities Management course: weekly article analysis, Togal.AI estimating assignments, and my final research project on AI adoption in the AEC industry.

Project 01

Weekly Article Collection

This project includes my weekly article summaries and presentations for the AI in Construction and Facilities Management course. The articles covered AI in gaming, education risks, AI regulation, AI agents, BIM integration, data center policy, investor safeguards, and responsible AI governance.

Through this collection, I connected broader AI developments to construction topics such as safety, workforce impacts, automation, ethics, project management, and future AEC technology.

Project 02

AI for Estimating: Togal.AI Assignments

This project focused on experimenting with Togal.AI to explore the capabilities, strengths, and limitations of AI-powered estimating tools. The assignment used construction drawing information to understand how AI can support quantity takeoff and estimating workflows.

The project helped me evaluate where AI can save time in preconstruction and estimating, while also showing why human review is still important for drawing interpretation, accuracy, and professional judgment.

Project 03

Final Project: AI Adoption in the AEC Industry

My final project, titled Navigating the New Frontier: AI Adoption in the AEC Industry, explored how engineering professionals are currently using AI in their work. The project was based on virtual/email interviews with professionals in bridge/structural engineering and transportation engineering.

The research found that AI is being used for documentation, data analysis, coding support, dashboards, visualization, structural analysis support, and workflow efficiency. It also emphasized human-in-the-loop decision-making, data quality, organizational support, and responsible AI adoption.
